Detailed Description

Phase change specific heat model.

The phase change specific heat \((c^{*}_\mathrm{p})\) model takes into account the phase change of a material by considering the latent heat into the specific heat over a phase change temperature interval determined by \(\left[T_\mathrm{s},T_\mathrm{l}\right]\) where \(T_\mathrm{s}\) is the solidus temperature and \(T_\mathrm{l}\) is the liquidus temperature.

The specific heat is evaluated as: \( c^{*}_\mathrm{p}(T) = \begin{cases} C_\mathrm{p,s} & \text{if} \; T<T_\mathrm{s}\\ \frac{C_\mathrm{p,s}+C_\mathrm{p,l}}{2}+\frac{h_\mathrm{l}} {T_\mathrm{l}-T_\mathrm{s}} & \text{if} \; T\in[T_\mathrm{s},T_\mathrm{l}]\\ C_\mathrm{p,l} & \text{if} \; T>T_\mathrm{l} \end{cases} \)

where \(C_\mathrm{p,s}\) and \(C_\mathrm{p,l}\) are the solid and liquid specific heat, respectively, and \(h_\mathrm{l}\) is the latent enthalpy (enthalpy related to the phase change).

double PhaseChangeSpecificHeat::enthalpy ( const double  T)
inline

Compute the enthalpy of a phase change material for a temperature \(T\).

The enthalpy \((H)\) is defined:

  • In pure liquid \((T>T_\mathrm{l})\) as:
    \(H(T) = c_\mathrm{p,s} T_\mathrm{s} + 0.5(c_\mathrm{p,s}+c_\mathrm{p,l}) (T_\mathrm{l}-T_\mathrm{s}) + h_\mathrm{l} + c_\mathrm{p,l} (T-T_\mathrm{l})\)

  • In liquid-solid mixture \((T_\mathrm{s} \leq T \leq T_\mathrm{l})\) as:
    \(H(T) = c_\mathrm{p,s} T_\mathrm{s} + 0.5(c_\mathrm{p,s}+c_\mathrm{p,l}) (T_\mathrm{l}-T_\mathrm{s}) + \alpha_\mathrm{l} h_\mathrm{l}\)
    where \(\alpha_\mathrm{l} = \frac{T-T_\mathrm{s}}{T_\mathrm{l}- T_\mathrm{s}}\) is the liquid fraction.

  • In pure solid \((T<T_\mathrm{s})\) as:
    \(H(T) = c_\mathrm{p,s} T\)
Parameters
[in]Ttemperature at which the enthalpy is computed.
Returns
Value of the enthalpy evaluated at \(T\).
